Making 3D models isn’t just hitting a button—it’s a full creative and technical process. First, I start with concept art or references to guide the design. Then, I block out the basic shape in software like Maya or 3ds Max before sculpting the details. After that, I create textures—basically the skin, color, and surface detail that make the model feel alive. I also add materials so things like metal shine or fabric looks soft. Once that’s done, I optimize the model so it runs smoothly in a game engine like Unreal or Unity. Every step takes patience, problem-solving, and a good eye for detail, but that’s what makes a character, weapon, or environment go from an idea to something players can actually interact with
